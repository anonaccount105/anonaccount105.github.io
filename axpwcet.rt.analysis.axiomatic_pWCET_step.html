In this file, we prove Theorem 1 presented in the paper "A Rigorous Axiomatic Definition of pWCET".

Step-by-step Proof of Theorem 1

In this section, we present the proof of Theorem 1 from the paper "A Rigorous Axiomatic Definition of pWCET". Due to the length of the proof and the nature of Coq, we cannot start this section with the statement of the theorem. Instead, we will first prove many theorems and then combine them together for a complete proof. Readers who would like to see the formal statement of the theorem first are invited to section ProofOfTheorem1.

In the upcoming section, we demonstrate a step-by-step proof of the main claim. It is important to note that Coq's preferred method of proof is a "bottom-up" approach, while humans are better suited to understanding the top-down explanations. In other words, in Coq it is easier to present proofs in the following way: first simple facts are proven, then one can use these simple facts to construct more sophisticated facts, then even more sophisticated, and so on until the final goal is reached. Yet, a reasonable question for a human would be "where is this going?".
Therefore, we adopt a more paper-like approach in which we present the proof as a series of implications: C ‚Üí Goal, B ‚Üí C, A ‚Üí B. This allows us to begin with the final goal (assuming that some facts are given) and then gradually work our way towards the "leafs" with specific details.

Step 1

Now we are ready to start the proof. First, we do the case analysis on all possible arrival sequences.
    Section Step1.

Consider a partition Œæpart of the sample space Œ©_of S of system S into events corresponding to different arrival sequences. Indices of partition Œæpart are arrival sequences themselves.
Here Œæpart is an (indexed) set of events, where an Œæi-th event denoted as Œæpart‚óÅ{Œæi} represents a subset of Œ© where the arrival sequence is equal to Œæi.
      Let Œæpart := partition_on_Œæ Œº.

As discussed in the paper, one can extend partition Œæpart to system S' and denote it as Œæpart'. For more detail see function partition_transfer/extend_partition.

Partitions Œæpart and Œæpart' are so similar that one can prove equivalence. For example, two indices Œæi and Œæi' that are "pickle equivalent" can be proved to be the same. (Pickle equivalence is a very strong type of equivalence, one can intuitively view it simply as an equality between two elements of similar types; for a def see file util/big_inf.v).

        ‚àÄ (Œæi : I Œæpart) (Œæi' : I Œæpart') (Œæ_equivalence : pickle_bij Œæi Œæi'),
        Œæi = Œæi'.

Now, recall that we present the proof in a top-bottom (C ‚Üí Goal) fashion.
So, assuming that for any two elements of partitions Œæi : I Œæpart and Œæi' : I Œæpart' that are "pickle"-equivalent, it holds that ‚Ñô{[ Exc1 ‚à© Œæpart‚óÅ{Œæi} ]} ‚â§ ‚Ñô{[ Exc2 ‚à© Œæpart'‚óÅ{Œæi'} ]}, ...

        ‚àÄ (Œæi : I Œæpart) (Œæi' : I Œæpart') (ŒæEQU : pickle_bij Œæi Œæi'),
          ‚Ñô<Œº_of S>{[ Exc ‚à© Œæpart‚óÅ{Œæi} ]} ‚â§ ‚Ñô<Œº_of S'>{[ Exc' ‚à© Œæpart'‚óÅ{Œæi'} ]}.

... we can show that ‚Ñô<Œº_of S>{[ Exc1 ]} ‚â§ ‚Ñô<Œº_of S'>{[ Exc2 ]}. Or, in other words, we reduced the lemma statement to the hypothesis statement.
      Lemma transformation_is_pRT_monotone_step1 :
        ‚Ñô<Œº_of S>{[ Exc ]} ‚â§ ‚Ñô<Œº_of S'>{[ Exc' ]}.

    End Step1.

Step 2

Now, we know that if we have the inequality with partitions on arrival sequences H_ineq_Œæ_partitioned, then we are done. But how do we prove such an inequality? In the next step, we prove the required inequality by introducing new hypothesis (we continue until the hypothesis becomes easy enough to prove it without a new hypothesis).
During this step, we replace the partition over all arrival sequences with an event encoding one arrival sequence.
    Section Step2.

First, let us state the premise of our hypothesis H_ineq_Œæ_partitioned.
Again, consider a partition Œæpart of the sample space of system S into events corresponding to different arrival sequences...

... and Œæpart to system S' and denote it as Œæpart'.

As it was discussed the pickle-equivalence is quite strong and we can show that both indices Œæi and Œæi' correspond to the same arrival sequence Œæ. Because of the way partitions on arrival sequences are constructed, we can extract an arrival sequence by unpacking a partition.

Step 3

Now, we can forget about Œæpart and Œæpart' and use Œæ, Œæf, and Œæf' instead. Notice that here we use Œæ to denote the deterministic arrival sequence. Predicates Œæf and Œæf' denote events that Œæ appeared in system S and in system S', respectively.

During this step, we introduce partition (that guarantees partition-independence and partition-boundedness) of the probability space using the assumption of axiomatic pWCET.

Then, we can prove the inequality that we assumed in the previous step (Step2). Notice that in order to apply the conclusion of H_ineq_Œ©_part, we need to provide all the premises of the hypothesis.     Section Step4.

First, note that both Œæf ‚à© Sf and Œæf' ‚à© Sf' have the same probability. This is due to the fact that our transformation does not change the probability of arrival sequence and partitions.
